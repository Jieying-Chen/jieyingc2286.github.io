Research Interests

I am a PhD student at the University of Cambridge, passionate about pushing the boundaries of computer vision and graphics. My research focuses on 3D scene understanding, neural rendering, and efficient video generation techniques. I'm particularly interested in developing methods that bridge the gap between synthetic and real-world visual data, with applications in augmented reality, robotics, and digital content creation.

Currently, I'm exploring novel view synthesis, Gaussian splatting, and efficient neural representations for real-time rendering. I believe in creating technology that is not only powerful but also accessible and practical for real-world applications.
